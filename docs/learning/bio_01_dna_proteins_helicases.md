# Biology Part 1: DNA, Proteins, and Helicases

This section explains the basic biology behind the repo.

## DNA In Plain Language

DNA is the information-carrying molecule used by living systems.

A useful beginner picture is:
- DNA is like a long information string
- that string is written using a four-letter alphabet
- the letters are usually written as **A, T, C, G**

In many contexts, DNA exists as **double-stranded DNA**, often shortened to **dsDNA**.

That means:
- there are two strands
- the strands pair together
- the paired structure forms the famous double-helix idea

## What “30 Base Pairs” Means

This repo uses a short DNA segment described as **30 bp**.

“bp” means **base pairs**.

So this is a short double-stranded DNA fragment with 30 paired positions.

It is short enough to handle conveniently in a modeling workflow, but long enough to show meaningful placement relative to the protein ring.

## Proteins In Plain Language

Proteins are biological machines built from amino acids.

Some proteins:
- provide structure
- carry signals
- catalyze chemical reactions
- bind or move along DNA

A protein’s behavior depends strongly on its 3D shape. That is why structure files matter so much in molecular modeling.

## What A Helicase Is

A **helicase** is a protein machine that interacts with nucleic acids and is often involved in separating, moving along, loading onto, or remodeling DNA-related structures.

For a beginner, the important idea is:
- a helicase is not just “touching” DNA
- it is a mechanical protein that often works with DNA geometry directly

That is why a ring-like helicase model naturally raises questions like:
- is the DNA centered?
- does it pass through the channel?
- is the geometry plausible?

## Why Ring Shape Matters

Many biological assemblies are not shaped like solid balls. Some are open, some are elongated, and some form rings or channels.

If a protein forms a ring or ring-like assembly, then placement of DNA relative to the center becomes a major structural question.

That is exactly the kind of question the visuals in this repo are helping answer.

## The Important Beginner Takeaway

You do not need to know advanced molecular biology yet.

For this repo, the essential biology is:

- DNA is the strand-like molecule of interest
- proteins are 3D molecular machines
- helicases are DNA-related protein machines
- Twinkle is being modeled as a ring-like complex interacting with DNA
